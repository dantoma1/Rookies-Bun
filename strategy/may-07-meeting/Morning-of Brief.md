# Open with this — and then stop talking

> *"Mick, thank you for the time. Cristian, thanks for setting this up. Before I show you anything, I'd rather understand your situation first. You've worked with JobTeaser long enough to know exactly where it works and where it doesn't. I'd love to hear that perspective before I tell you anything about Rookies."*

Take out the notebook. Wait. *Listening before pitching is the highest-leverage move you make.* See *Strategic Briefing* §V on enrollment.

# The narrative — three pillars, three audiences

**Students.** A discovery surface that presents every job by relevance, surfaces SMEs other platforms hide, stays with them past placement. Mobile-native, profile-driven, no opt-in walls.

**Companies.** Recruiters reach the right TiU students directly: ranked, transparent matches with one-click invite-to-apply. Small employers post freely without artificial caps. The platform Brabant boutique firms can actually use.

**TiU career services — the audience that matters most for this meeting.** A *Career Officer Console* designed for Mick's team's daily work. The Match Analyzer turns 1-on-1 sessions into coached conversations. Market Pulse keeps officers current on what employers want. The Engagement view flags at-risk students for proactive outreach. **The platform doesn't replace the human work — it makes it sharper.** Memorize that sentence. It directly answers Mick's email.

# What separates Rookies — and what JobTeaser does well

| **Rookies' five structural advantages** | **What JobTeaser does well today** |
|---|---|
| **1. Matching at the core.** Bidirectional, available to every user. Built in across the platform. | **Operational back-office.** Real human support team. |
| **2. Skills-gap intelligence for TiU's management.** Real-time demand vs. supply — supports accreditation, rankings, positioning. | **Events module.** Career fairs, registration, attendance. |
| **3. SME accessibility.** Free unlimited posting fixes the "twenty companies" problem at its source. | **ATS integrations.** Kombo connects to 100+ recruiter systems. |
| **4. Direct outreach + Career Officer Console.** Coached 1-on-1s, ranked-candidate discovery, no Talent Bank walls. | **Orientation tools** (Marco, Freya). |
| **5. Longitudinal outcome tracking.** Placement signal continues past application — feeds TiU's curriculum data over years. | |

If pressed on any parity item: *"That's exactly why a parallel pilot makes sense — JobTeaser keeps doing what it does well; we focus on the gaps it leaves open."* Then *Q&A Playbook* §VI for depth.

# Things you do NOT volunteer

- **Long-term commercial model** — *"a conversation for after September."* (*Q&A* §IX)
- **JobTeaser's 800-university network** — their market position, not yours. Citing it shrinks you.
- **Team size, funding, equity** — *"the founding team."* Move on. (*Briefing* §IV)
- **Promises to build events / ATS / scheduling** — parallel-pilot reframe instead.
- **The €5–10k post-September university pricing** — premature anchor; deflect.

# The closing ask — small, safe, specific

The only commitment Mick should be asked for is whether he'd take this to Herbert as a conversation. Not endorsement. *Open the next door.* That's a yes he can say without feeling exposed.

# When X happens, remember Y

- **He defends JobTeaser** → *"You should be a fan — by your criteria, it works. We're not asking you to switch."* Reframe to parallel pilot.
- **"I don't see a distinguishing factor"** → demo the Match Analyzer first, Career Officer Console second. Visual > verbal.
- **He pushes on operational support** → *"At pilot scale, the back office is me. Past pilot, if it's working, we hire."*
- **Events / scheduling / ATS** → *"Those aren't workflows we're trying to compete on."* Parallel pilot.
- **"We have processes built around JobTeaser"** → *"Exactly why a parallel pilot makes sense — your workflows don't change."*
- **"What's your business model?"** → *"That's a conversation for after September. Forcing a number now gives you a worse negotiating position than starting fresh."*
- **"Let me think about it"** → *"The question to answer for yourself is whether the pilot has bounded enough downside that exploration is worth it — not whether you're confident we'll deliver on every promise."*
- **"What would you need from me?"** → *"Willingness to take this to Herbert as a 30-minute conversation. That's it."*

# The four principles to hold in mind

1. **Make Mick the hero.** He's the External Affairs Officer who found Rookies and brought it forward — not the buyer, the discoverer.
2. **Honor his philosophy.** Career services is about 1-on-1 human work. Rookies makes it sharper, never claims to replace it.
3. **Listen for what he's not saying.** Qualifiers like *"although sometimes…"* are where the real frustrations live. Note them. Cite them back.
4. **You're pitching value, not seeking approval.** His personal opinion of you is not on the table. Stay disconnected from his emotional reaction; it makes you sharper.

**Get a low-commitment yes. That's the whole game.**
